    Hurricane season (Jun-Nov)

    Risk window: June through November overlaps with the strongest revenue stretch, so operational resilience matters most when sales opportunity is highest.
    • Pre-stage weather messaging and refund/change policies before coastal storm watches.
    • Use POS-linked demand snapshots to compare planned labor against likely reduced foot traffic when severe weather disrupts beach visitation.
    • Protect inventory and outdoor seating plans in July-September, when rainfall is highest in the supplied monthly normal set and tropical activity is a recurring operational risk.
    • Maintain a storm-week promo playbook for locals, carryout, and televised events to transfer demand indoors.

    Investor narrative

    Business model

    HopLite becomes a seasonal operating intelligence layer for hospitality: planning, reserve management, staffing design, promo attribution, and investor reporting in one productized workflow.

    Moat

    The moat is local-seasonality intelligence plus embedded operating habits. Once revenue pacing, promo learning, staffing templates, and weather-response logic live in one system, replacement cost rises.

    Why software, not a spreadsheet

    This experience links assumptions to actions. Operators can demo changes live, while investors see repeatable process, not just a static forecast.

    Integration story

    • Toast serves as the logical POS anchor for future actual-sales ingestion.
    • The workflow can extend into accounting, labor, online ordering, and campaign measurement feeds.
    • That makes the next step credible: replace sample inputs with operator-grade signals.

    Investor takeaway

    This is not just “restaurant software.” It is a seasonal cash-defense and demand-orchestration system for coastal hospitality businesses, starting with one venue and expanding into a repeatable operating model.

    How to Use

    Condensed walkthrough for meetings, demos, and investor conversations.

    Tab guide

    • Overview: start here; edit peak revenue, overhead, and cushion live.
    • Seasonality: adjust monthly revenue values directly in the table.
    • Monthly Drilldown: pick a month and talk through actions, weather, and staffing.
    • Forecast vs Actual: edit actuals to demonstrate operator accountability.
    • Promo Log: explain how learning compounds through campaign ROI.
    • Team/Shifts: show labor changing with demand, not staying static.
    • Weather: reinforce storm and precipitation planning.
    • Investor View: close on product thesis and scale path.

    Suggested meeting flow

    1. Open on Overview and adjust the top-line inputs.
    2. Move to Seasonality and edit monthly revenue values live.
    3. Use Monthly Drilldown and Team/Shifts with the same month selected.
    4. Edit actual sales and promo values to prove the score is live.
    5. Finish on Investor View to reframe this as software, not just analysis.
    Best live controls to edit: peak monthly revenue, overhead, cushion %, monthly revenue inputs, forecast actuals, and promo spend / incremental sales.
